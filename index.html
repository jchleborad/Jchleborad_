Chleborad [ kleb' -or-ad]

Origin: Bohemian

Native Language of Bohemia: Czech

Meaning: Bread

Chleborad, therefore, would refer to a baker or one who works with or enjoys bread

At one time Bohemia had been part of the Holy Roman Empire. It was bordered by Austria, Bavaria and Saxony to name a few. After WWI it became part of Czechoslovakia and between 1938 & 1945 was joined with Germany as part of a region called Sudetenland. In 1969 the Czech lands were renamed the Czech Socialist Republic and in 1990 became the Czech Republic
